Overview

Problem

Users were overwhelmed by the vast amount of disorganized information on the site.

Solution

Introduce a new navigation system and redesign energy.gov with a modern twist

Redesigning both the user interface and the information architecture was a challenge. With user tests and navigation tests, our team discovered that users had difficulty navigating the site, often getting frustrated and leaving. A heuristic evaluation and annotations of our main pages helped us determine the fundamental problems we needed to fix in the redesign.

Intuitive Navigation

Through testing and card sorting we ware able to fully grasp the size of this site and give it a more logical organization.

Modernize the brand

The brand redesign was inspired by a user quote - when asked what energy means to them they replied, "energy is the difference between light and dark.

Mobile friendly redesign

Our approach was to focus on mobile first. We made sure the content flowed on mobile devices as well as it did on full sized browsers.

Eliminate repeating content

While sorting through the pages we discovered some repeating information.

Conducting Interviews with visitors to the site while watching them navigate the site led us to conclude the DoE’s primary users go to the website for a specific reason. Due to the immensity of the site, they often rely entirely on the search function to find the information they need. This finding led us to redesign the home page with the search bar featured in a primary and centered location.

Redesign Inspiration

The idea for the redesign comes to form a user interview. "Energy to me is about light and dark, warm, and cold." That simple sentence captured the essence of energy, in our opinion, so we decided to center the design to this statement. The design is clean, black, and white.

Annotations & Heuristic Analisys

To better understand the frustration of the users.

We discovered layout inconsistency throughout different sections of the site. There are four different menu systems with no clear indication on how to go to the starting point. The site is massive in content with multiple micro sites catering to a wide variety of users. But yet the search option is almost hidden.

Usability Testing & Color Testing

We conducted sevral Users tests and we quickly realize that the main frustration was with navigation
